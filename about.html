Our Ethiopian Heritage & Traditional Food Journey

From Ethiopia to East York Toronto

The story of Sora begins in the highlands of Ethiopia, where teff grain has been cultivated for over 3,000 years. Our founder grew up in a family that maintained traditional Ethiopian cooking methods, learning the art of fermenting teff to create perfect injera, the cornerstone of Ethiopian cuisine.

Moving to East York Toronto, we recognized the need for authentic Ethiopian food that honored traditional preparation methods while serving the diverse Danforth area community. We committed to sourcing only the highest quality organic teff and indigenous Ethiopian spices to recreate the authentic flavors of our homeland.

Ethiopian Coffee Ceremony - A Cultural Experience

Authentic Coffee Culture in the Heart of East York Toronto

Coffee originated in Ethiopia, and the traditional coffee ceremony is an integral part of Ethiopian culture. At Sora, we honor this heritage by offering authentic coffee ceremonies that bring people together in the spirit of community and connection.

Our coffee ceremony begins with washing and roasting green coffee beans over an open flame, filling the air with aromatic coffee fragrance. The beans are then ground by hand and brewed in a traditional clay pot called a jebena. This process creates not just exceptional coffee, but a meditative experience that embodies Ethiopian hospitality.
